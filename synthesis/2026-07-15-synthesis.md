## KRMKF (Kromek Group plc) — WATCH (timing-sensitive)

### Signal: MODERATE
The order momentum is **real, specific, and cash-backed — but its headline strength is transient.** It passes all three quality tests on the recurring core, then fails the durability test on the headline.

- **Real (recurring core):** CBRN Detection H1 FY2026 revenue £4.3m, **+115% YoY** from £2.0m. This is genuine product revenue from defense/security government customers with zero one-time distortion — every pound recurring. Underlying Advanced Imaging ex-Siemens £2.5m, **+41% YoY**. These are recognized, not pipeline.
- **Specific:** H2 FY2026 new orders **confirmed at £8.8m**. FY2026 full-year guidance £27.2m revenue (+240%) and **£2.15m PBT — the first profitable year in company history.** Named framework coverage (UK Home Office, US DoD) gives structured forward order visibility.
- **Cash-backed but front-loaded:** The £15.0m H1 headline (+305%) is inflated by the Siemens Healthineers enablement payment ($37.5m deal). That cash is real, but the recognition schedule is a cliff: **FY2025 £16.5m → FY2026 ~£9.3m → FY2027 ~£2.0m.** Strip Siemens and the durable recurring base is small (£4.3m CBRN + £2.5m underlying imaging).

The clean signal (CBRN +115%, cash-backed government revenue) is genuine and durable; the headline signal (+305%, +240% FY) is milestone-distorted and mathematically reverses in FY2027. That split — real core, transient headline — is Moderate, not Strong. The asymmetry is live **now**, while FY2026 numbers are high, and decays as the cliff comes into view.

### Moat: QUALIFIED (CBRN) / ERODING-COMPETING (medical CZT)
The moat is split, and — unlike TRT yesterday — the split runs the *right* way: the durable moat sits under the durable revenue.

- **CBRN — Qualified (durable):** Pre-qualified across **all three categories** (handheld, wearable, large static) on the UK Government Radiological Nuclear Detection Framework, **£84m ceiling / 4yr**. Pre-qualified on the US DoD CBRN IDIQ, **$8.2bn / 10yr**. Government CBRN qualification requires MIL-spec testing, security clearance, and an **18–36 month requalification cycle** for any new entrant. "One of a limited number of pre-approved suppliers … uniquely positioned to strengthen national security." This is a real, structural, multi-year franchise.
- **Medical CZT — Eroding toward Competing:** Management claims sole "independent commercial-scale CZT producer globally" — a monopoly claim. But the Siemens Enablement Agreement **transfers 15 of 174 furnaces plus proprietary CZT manufacturing IP to Siemens**, manufacturing a competitor out of the largest customer. Canon and others are entering photon-counting CT with their own CZT. The imaging moat is being sold off — almost certainly a capital-for-IP survival trade.

Net: **Qualified.** The durable edge is CBRN, and CBRN is where the durable revenue lives. The medical monopoly is a depreciating asset the company is actively liquidating; discount it toward zero as a moat.

### (a) What keeps institutions out NOW — specific
Four stacked structural barriers, none of them valuation:

1. **Dual-listing exclusion (AIM + OTC Pink Sheet).** The binding constraint. US institutions cannot hold OTC Pink positions under most fund mandates; UK institutions largely require FTSE inclusion, which an AIM name lacks. Kromek falls into the gap between both regimes — structurally unbuyable by US institutions and most UK ones simultaneously. This is a *venue* gate, not a coverage gate.
2. **Zero sell-side coverage.** No initiation → no price target → no Street model → no institutional research memo. Funds with a "must be covered" gate have no compliance pathway to act, even where the venue permits.
3. **Milestone-accounting distortion.** The Siemens tail (£16.5m → £9m → £2m) makes the P&L unreadable to any standard screen: FY2026 reads as a one-time spike and earns a low-quality multiple, while the +115% CBRN growth is buried behind the noise. Quant screens see distortion, not signal.
4. **GICS classification void.** "CZT radiation detector" maps to instruments / medical devices — not defense, not semiconductor, not specialty materials. It appears on **no institutional thematic screen at all.** Layered on prior-year operating losses (FY2026 is the first profitable year), which kept value investors out while momentum investors never found it.

### (b) Graduation trigger — when institutions CAN buy (= re-rating event)
Two independent unlock paths; either one is a re-rating:

- **Analyst initiation (the compliance unlock).** A £70M-cap, £27m-revenue name with UK + US government defense qualification is an obvious candidate for a Shard / Shore / Canaccord small-cap initiation. One initiating note creates a price target and satisfies coverage gates → re-rate from <3x toward 4–5x EV/Sales. **This is the target exit for pre-initiation holders.**
- **A material framework drawdown that proves CBRN scales (the fundamental unlock).** Individual task orders under the US DoD $8.2bn IDIQ or the UK Home Office £84m framework are each an RNS event. **Any single award >£3m is material for a £70M-cap company** and — critically — demonstrates the CBRN engine is ramping fast enough to offset the Siemens cliff. That is the datapoint that converts "framework ceiling" into "committed revenue."
- **Venue mechanical unlock (highest leverage, lowest probability near-term):** a US exchange uplisting off Pink Sheets, or FTSE inclusion, would mechanically open the institutional door that items 1 and 3–4 above only work around.

**Forcing event:** FY2026 Full Year Results, expected **September 2026 (~8 weeks)** — first annual showing full-year £27.2m, first profit, full CBRN segment disclosure, and the initial read on whether FY2027 CBRN drawdowns offset the Siemens tail.

### (c) What kills the thesis
- **The FY2027 Siemens revenue cliff — the primary kill.** Milestone revenue tails from ~£9m (FY2026) to ~£2m (FY2027). If CBRN framework drawdowns plus non-Siemens imaging wins do not offset, FY2027 guidance triggers a **downward** re-rating — and profitability likely reverses back to losses, re-alienating value and momentum buyers simultaneously. The entire bet is that CBRN growth outruns the cliff.
- **Frameworks are ceilings, not commitments.** £84m and $8.2bn are drawdown *maximums*. If task-order flow stays minimal, the "moat" produces qualification without revenue — a franchise on paper only.
- **Medical CZT moat fully competed away.** Siemens becomes a direct competitor via the 15-furnace/IP transfer; Canon enters. If the CZT position collapses faster than CBRN scales, the growth story loses its second leg.
- **Cap/venue drift.** At ~$89M USD the name sits mid-zone with room, but a US institutional pathway never opening means the asymmetry persists indefinitely with no catalyst — a value trap rather than a re-rating.

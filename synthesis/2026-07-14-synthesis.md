## CMLMF (CML Microsystems) — WATCH

**Signal: MODERATE.** H2 FY26 revenue £11.27m vs H1 £9.18m (+18% sequential, first YoY-positive half in cycle). Real inflection, but no disclosed backlog or book-to-bill — momentum is directional, not cash-quantified. FY26 headline revenue still -11% YoY masks the H2 turn.

**Moat: QUALIFIED.** 12-year, $30M+ GNSS design-and-supply agreement with "stringent manufacturing requirements" implies functional sole-source design-in lock-in. Not a monopoly — product revenue is 2–3 years out (FY28–29), so the moat is contracted but unrealized.

Moderate signal against a qualified (non-monopoly) moat lands short of highest-conviction; the driver is timing, hence timing-sensitive WATCH.

**(a) What keeps institutions out now:** Structural exclusion, not valuation. ~$56M cap, <$400K/day volume (4–5x under threshold), AIM/Pink Sheets access, 3 UK analysts and zero US coverage. No fund >$50–100M AUM can build a position. EV ~$42M leaves the $30M+ contract effectively unpriced.

**(b) Graduation trigger:** FY27 H1 interim results (Oct/Nov 2026). A confirmed YoY-positive half forces analyst upgrades, potentially draws first US coverage, and — combined with rising volume — lifts the liquidity gate that currently blocks buyers. That is the re-rating moment.

**(c) What kills the thesis:** GNSS product revenue slips beyond FY29; H2 sequential gain proves one-off rather than an inflection; illiquidity persists so no institution can act even if the fundamentals confirm. Pre-tax loss (-£0.07m FY26) is small and improving, so P&L is not the primary risk — realization timing is.

None + Unknown is the textbook PASS quadrant. Two analysts carry $10.75–$14.00 targets vs a $4.62 price — a spec valuation with no order or moat evidence beneath it. First B-TRAN prototype (Q4 2026, Asia SSCB customer) is technology validation only, not an earnings catalyst. Revisit only if a prototype converts to a paid, deposit-backed order.

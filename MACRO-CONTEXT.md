# Macro Context — Semiconductor Equipment Cycle

Last updated: 2026-07-13

## WFE Cycle State

**CY2026 WFE spend consensus: ~$140–150B** (record cycle)
- Stifel: $145–150B; Citi bull-case: $145B; Lam Research guided industry at $140B
- Global WFE projected +21.4% YoY in 2026, +18.2% in 2027 (Bernstein)
- Citi raised PTs: AMAT $550→$710, LRCX $315→$450, KLAC $206→$290
- Cantor and Susquehanna also raised equipment spending forecasts mid-2026
- AMAT CEO: leading-edge foundry logic, DRAM (incl. HBM), and advanced packaging are "fastest-growing WFE segments in 2026, 2027, and for the next several years"
- Characterization: structural AI-driven demand, not cyclical recovery — sustained into 2027

## Customer Capex Signals

- **TSMC**: CY2026 capex $52–56B (+27–37% YoY); market expects possible upward revision to ~$70B
- **Samsung**: $73B total investment (semiconductor capex ~$40B, +20% YoY) — single largest annual semi investment in history
- **SK Hynix**: capex +40%+ YoY in 2026; HBM3E/HBM4 capacity expansion driving
- **Intel**: CY2026 capex flat-to-down from $17.7B in 2025; restructuring underway; net negative vs. peers
- Net read: TSMC + Samsung + SK Hynix collectively the largest equipment spenders, all accelerating; Intel the lone drag

## Sector Multiples

- Semiconductor equipment (8–12x EBITDA) commands highest multiple among industrial manufacturing sub-sectors in 2026
- Broader semis industry: ~7.57x EV/Sales, 17.8x EV/EBITDA (CSIMarket Q1 2026)
- Small/mid-cap equipment names ($100M–$4B): estimated 1.5–4x EV/Sales; names below 2x EV/Sales with improving order signals represent the asymmetry zone
- Book-to-bill ratios above 1.10 are widespread across sector; names with BTB above 1.20+ and sub-$500M cap are the target cohort

## Consensus Narrative

- **Prevailing view**: WFE upcycle is broad-based and well-telegraphed; sell-side universally bullish on large-caps (AMAT: 14/14 firms bullish, 0 holds/sells)
- **Cycle peak timing**: DRAM/HBM peak expected ~Q1 2027 (wide band H2 2026 – H2 2027); foundry logic sustained through 2027+
- **Edge thesis implications**: Large-cap equipment extensively covered and priced. Asymmetry requires: (a) niche sub-system suppliers not labeled AI plays, (b) companies below 4 sell-side analysts, (c) fresh IPOs with one-time accounting distortions, (d) sub-$500M market caps where supply chain demand signals precede revenue recognition
- **Tariff/geopolitical risk**: China-related export controls continue to cap China WFE exposure; companies with <20% China revenue structurally better positioned

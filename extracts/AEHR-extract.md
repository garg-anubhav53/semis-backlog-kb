## (e) MD&A Paragraphs — Orders, Bookings, and Backlog Outlook

**Source:** 8-K Exhibit 99.1 press release (April 7, 2026, accession 000165495426003310); language is substantially reproduced in the 10-Q MD&A "Orders and Backlog" section. The following passages are verbatim from the press release as indexed by SEC EDGAR full-text search and web search snippets.

> "We received net bookings of $37.2 million in the fiscal third quarter of 2026, resulting in a book-to-bill ratio of greater than 3.5x. Our bookings in the fiscal third quarter were driven by demand for both our FOX-XP Wafer Level Burn-In (WLBI) systems from hyperscale data center customers for artificial intelligence processors and for our Sonoma Product Level Burn-In (PLBI) systems from customers producing silicon photonics optical transceivers for high-speed data center interconnects."

> "Our backlog as of February 27, 2026 was $38.7 million, compared to $11.8 million as of November 28, 2025. Effective backlog, which includes our backlog of $38.7 million at the end of our fiscal third quarter, plus additional bookings of $12.2 million received since the end of the quarter, is $50.9 million, a new Company record."

> "Due to the possibility of customer changes in delivery schedules or cancellations and potential delays in product shipments or development projects, our backlog as of a particular date may not be indicative of net revenues for any succeeding period."

CEO Gayn Erickson quote from press release (also cited in 10-Q context):

> "We are pleased to report that our third quarter bookings of $37.2 million represent the highest quarterly bookings in our history and are a strong indicator of the significant demand we are seeing from our customers for our semiconductor test and burn-in systems. The record $50.9 million effective backlog further demonstrates the confidence our customers have in Aehr as a critical partner to support their growing AI and data center semiconductor production needs."

## (f) Revenue Recognition Policy

**Source:** Notes to Condensed Consolidated Financial Statements, Note 2 "Revenue Recognition" (or "Summary of Significant Accounting Policies"), as indexed from the 10-Q filing via SEC EDGAR full-text search. Language is consistent with prior Aehr Test filings.

> "Revenue is recognized when control of the promised goods or services is transferred to our customers, in an amount that reflects the consideration we expect to be entitled to in exchange for those goods or services."

Additional policy language (consistent across filings):

> "For product sales, we recognize revenue generally at the time of product delivery, or in certain instances, upon customer acceptance, which is when control is deemed to have transferred to the customer. For service revenue, we recognize revenue ratably over the service period as services are delivered."

**Location in filing:** Note 2, "Revenue," within Notes to Condensed Consolidated Financial Statements.

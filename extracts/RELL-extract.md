## Backlog Definition

From 10-Q MD&A (via press release and investor presentation): Backlog represents orders that have been received and accepted but not yet shipped or recognized as revenue. Backlog does not include shipments that have occurred but have not yet been invoiced.

From Note on Revenue Recognition (confirmed via web search of filing text): "The company receives advance payments or deposits from customers before revenue is recognized, resulting in contract liabilities that are included in accrued liabilities in the Consolidated Balance Sheets."

## Revenue Recognition Policy

"Revenue is recognized when control of promised goods or services is transferred to customers in an amount that reflects the consideration to which the entity expects to be entitled in exchange for those goods or services." Advance payments received prior to delivery are recorded as contract liabilities within accrued liabilities on the Consolidated Balance Sheet until revenue recognition criteria are met upon shipment or delivery.

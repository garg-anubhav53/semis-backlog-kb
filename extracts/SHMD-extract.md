## 1. Backlog / Order Book Definition

From Q1 2026 6-K (April 27, 2026):
"Order intake and order book figures relate exclusively to orders for equipment and do not include orders associated with services or spare parts."

From Q4 2025 earnings call (management quote):
"SCHMID receives a 30% down payment from customers, produces the equipment, and recognizes revenue when it ships the equipment."

"Orders taken until July or August will still be recognized as revenue in the fiscal year, provided they can be produced and shipped before year-end."

The company uses two key metrics:
- **Order intake**: new orders received in the reporting period
- **Order book** (equivalent to backlog): cumulative orders received but not yet recognized as revenue

## 2. Order Book / Backlog Figures

**Full Year 2025:**
"SCHMID ended 2025 with order intake of more than EUR 90 million and a very healthy order book of EUR 51 million."

**Q1 2026 (period ended March 31, 2026):**
- Q1 2026 order intake: **€13.6 million**
- Q1 2026 revenue: **€18.2 million** (preliminary, unaudited)
- **Order book at end of Q1 2026: €49 million**

**H1 2026 (six months ended June 30, 2026) — from July 7, 2026 6-K:**
- "Order intake in the six months ended June 30, 2026 was EUR 44.3 million excluding this order and **EUR 81.7 million including it.**"

- H1 2026 order intake of €81.7M represents **71.7% of FY2026 order intake guidance (€114M) already achieved in first half.**

Implied Q2 2026 order intake: €81.7M − €13.6M (Q1) = **€68.1M** (single quarter; includes the €37M+ repeat order).

## 4. Customer Deposits / Advance Payments

From Q4 2025 earnings call (management statement):
"When selling machines, customers' payments are typically divided into an advanced payment on receipt of order confirmation, an advance payment upon delivery and, when installation services are requested, a final payment after installation and customer acceptance of the services."

"SCHMID typically receives about 30% of cash advance from customers on most equipment orders, which helps to keep the working capital at a reasonable level."

Implication: the €37M+ repeat order (received in Q2 2026) triggered a cash advance of approximately **€11M+** to SCHMID's balance sheet in Q2 2026.

## 6. Revenue Recognition Policy

From Q4 2025 earnings call management statement (verbatim):
"SCHMID receives a 30% down payment from customers, produces the equipment, and recognizes revenue when it ships the equipment. Orders taken until July or August will still be recognized as revenue in the fiscal year, provided they can be produced and shipped before year-end."

Revenue recognition = upon shipment (point-in-time, consistent with IFRS 15 / ASC 606 for equipment manufacturers). 30% advance payment received at order confirmation → recognized as deferred revenue (contract liability) until shipment.

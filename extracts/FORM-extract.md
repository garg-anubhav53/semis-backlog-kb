## 1. Backlog Definition

FormFactor does not define or disclose a formal "backlog" figure in its 10-Q. The company refers instead to "capacity booked" and "orders scheduled." From the 10-Q MD&A (per cited sources): management uses the language "our 2026 capacity has been fully booked, with orders scheduled through year-end" — indicating forward order coverage but not disclosed as a dollar backlog figure.

There is no GAAP-defined "backlog" metric in FormFactor's reporting.

## 2. Backlog / Order Figures

No specific dollar backlog is disclosed. Reported as operational status:

- **"FormFactor's 2026 capacity has been fully booked, with orders scheduled through year-end."**
- Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron "have already sold out their entire 2026 capacity and are taking orders for 2027."
- Q1 2026 revenue: **$226.1 million** (+32.0% YoY from $171.4M; +5.1% QoQ from $215.1M) — second consecutive all-time revenue record.
- Q2 2026 guidance: **$240 million ± $5 million** — which would be the third consecutive all-time record.
- "GPU-related revenue of approximately $20 million is still expected in the second half of the year."

Prior-quarter comparison: Q4 2025 revenue was approximately $215.1M (implied from +5.1% sequential growth to $226.1M in Q1 2026).

## 5. MD&A — Orders/Backlog (verbatim where attributed)

From Q1 2026 10-Q (as cited in multiple sources quoting the filing directly):

"The increase in DRAM product revenues for the three months ended March 28, 2026, was primarily driven by increased demand for high-bandwidth memory ('HBM') designs utilized in generative artificial intelligence applications."

From Q1 2026 earnings call (CEO Mike Slessor):
"FormFactor's first quarter revenue grew sequentially to the second consecutive all-time record, with gross margin and earnings per share significantly above the high end of our outlook range...These outstanding results exceed our target model on a quarterly run-rate basis and together with our current quarter outlook is expected to validate the model on an annualized basis."

On capacity: "Management acknowledged they are running at very high utilization and 'squeezing more juice' out of the existing footprint through yield and cycle time improvements, while significant capacity expansion is dependent on the 2027 ramp of the Texas [Farmers Branch] facility."

On demand horizon: major memory manufacturers (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron) "have already sold out their entire 2026 capacity and are taking orders for 2027."

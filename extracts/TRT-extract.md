## 1. Backlog / Orders Definition

The company distinguishes two categories of backlog:

**Equipment / Burn-In Board Manufacturing (Products):** Firm purchase orders with 12-month delivery windows. These are hard commitments.

**Testing Services:** Customer estimates only — NOT firm purchase orders. The company explicitly notes that testing services backlog figures carry inherent uncertainty because they are based on customer volume forecasts rather than binding commitments.

## 6. Sole-Source, Qualification, Supply Agreements, Proprietary Language

> "65+ years of proprietary testing methodologies" — cited by management in investor communications.

> "IATF 16949 certified" — automotive sector quality management standard certification. This is a real entry barrier for automotive semiconductor testing; competitors require 12-24+ months to achieve equivalent certification with a given customer.

> "Proprietary burn-in systems for high-reliability automotive applications" — company describes its burn-in hardware as proprietary, integrated into customer testing protocols for automotive-grade semiconductors.

**Geographic moat language:** Singapore/Malaysia presence cited as advantage for Asian semiconductor supply chain proximity (TSMC Malaysia, Infineon, etc.).

**No explicit sole-source language found.** No long-term supply agreement disclosures in publicly available excerpts. No DoD/MIL-SPEC/ITAR certifications mentioned. The IATF 16949 is the strongest qualification lock-in language found.

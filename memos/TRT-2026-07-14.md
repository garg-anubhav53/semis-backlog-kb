## 3. MOAT

**MOAT CLASSIFICATION: QUALIFIED (automotive segment) / COMPETING (AI GPU segment)**

**Evidence found:**

**AUTOMOTIVE MOAT — Real:**
> "IATF 16949 certified" — This is a binding automotive-sector quality management standard. Automotive OEMs and Tier 1 suppliers require IATF 16949 from all qualified test service providers. Achieving this certification with a specific automotive customer takes 12–24+ months and requires in-process audits, validated test methodologies, and documented traceability. Once Trio-Tech is the certified/qualified supplier for an automotive semiconductor device, switching to a competitor requires customer-initiated requalification — a process that interrupts production and is strongly disincentivized.

> "Proprietary burn-in systems for high-reliability automotive applications" — If burn-in hardware is customer-validated and embedded in production test flows, this extends lock-in.

> "65+ years of proprietary testing methodologies" — Institutional knowledge and validated process IP. Competitors do not have 65-year legacy of test data and methodology documentation.

**AI GPU MOAT — Unconfirmed:**
The $10.4M in burn-in board orders for "next-generation AI GPU platform" customers (North American and European) could reflect: (a) Trio-Tech as a qualified sole-source supplier to a specific GPU maker, or (b) Trio-Tech winning spot business in a competitive bidding process. No sole-source language, no qualification requirement language, no long-term supply agreement language was found for the AI GPU segment. This is the biggest moat gap.

**Moat summary:** AUTOMOTIVE = QUALIFIED (12-24mo requalification barrier confirmed via IATF 16949). AI GPU = UNKNOWN (opportunity more than structural moat currently). Full moat classification: **QUALIFIED** (with asterisk that the fastest-growing segment is the weakest moat segment).

**Specific reason signal is not in the price:** SIC 7389 + foreign HQ + AMEX listing. These three combined create a structural filter that most semi funds and quantitative screens cannot see through. Individual investors CAN see through it with primary SEC research.

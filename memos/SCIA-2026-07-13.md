## 3. MOAT: Is this company structurally hard to displace?

**Classification: QUALIFIED (requalification estimate 6-18 months)**

Evidence found:

**A. ISO 9001:2015 Certification (since 2010)**
ISO 9001:2015 is a quality management prerequisite for many defense and semiconductor supply chain customers. Without it, a supplier cannot be on approved vendor lists (AVLs). SCIA has held this continuously since April 2010.

**B. 5-Year Manufacturing Agreement — Battle Sight Technologies (June 2024)**
> "SCI Engineered Materials, Inc. Announces Manufacturing Agreement with Battle Sight Technologies" for ColdFIRE® photoluminescent powder for tactical defense equipment.
This is a contractual lock-in through at least 2029. The ColdFIRE® chemistry is proprietary to Battle Sight; SCIA is the contracted manufacturer. Neither party can easily substitute.

**C. Process Qualification Lock-in for PVD Targets**
SCI's own materials document states they have "enhanced production processes, increased manufacturing efficiencies, and testing equipment capabilities to enable it to manufacture and qualify many product compositions." The word "qualify" is deliberately technical — it refers to customer qualification runs at end-user facilities. Once a sputtering target supplier is qualified at a semiconductor fab (purity verification, defect density tests, process integration), the customer faces:
- 3-12 month re-qualification timeline with a new supplier
- Risk of yield loss during transition
- Process engineering hours to validate a substitute

**D. Toll Manufacturing Creates Proprietary Know-How**
"Toll manufacturing and related services revenue increased in 2025." In toll manufacturing, customers bring their proprietary materials to SCI for processing. SCI develops expertise specific to each customer's chemistry — which is non-transferable to a new supplier without significant transition cost.

**E. Enriched Boron Carbide for Nuclear/Aerospace**
This is a controlled/rare material requiring specific supplier qualifications in aerospace and nuclear supply chains. The fact that SCIA participates here suggests they hold materials-specific certifications beyond ISO 9001.

**Moat gap:** Explicit fab-level semiconductor approval language (process-of-record at TSMC/Samsung/etc.) was NOT found in available indexed text. This would be the strongest moat evidence. It may be in the 10-K Item 1 business description, which required direct EDGAR access (blocked in this session).

**Classification rationale:** The combination of ISO 9001:2015, toll manufacturing stickiness, 5-year contractual agreements, and PVD target qualification time justifies QUALIFIED moat. Not MONOPOLY (multiple sputtering target suppliers exist globally) but not COMPETING (structural switching costs are present).

## 4. ASYMMETRY: Why is this signal not in the price?

**Three compounding reasons:**

1. **GICS miscategorization.** SCI is classified as Specialty Chemicals, not Semiconductor Equipment & Materials. Any WFE-sector screen (Finviz, Bloomberg, FACTSET) using the semiconductor equipment industry filter will miss SCIA entirely. An analyst covering semiconductor supply chains via a standard sector screen never sees it.

2. **OTCQB listing.** Institutional mandates typically require NYSE or NASDAQ listing. OTCQB means:
   - Not in any major index
   - Not eligible for most ETFs
   - Not accessible via Bloomberg terminal equity basket screens
   - No clearing simplicity — requires special OTC trading workflow
   
3. **Zero sell-side coverage.** No EPS estimates exist. No analyst tracks the company. There is no formal research initiation that would price in the WFE cycle signal. The company has never appeared in a semiconductor equipment peer group.

4. **Ohio industrial company identity.** SCI presents itself as a materials manufacturer, not a semiconductor play. Their press releases (to the extent they issue them) use language like "physical vapor deposition materials supplier" — not "semiconductor supply chain." This framing further deflects attention from AI/WFE narrative investors.

**Bottom line:** The backlog +173% QoQ and revenue +133% YoY at a $30M cap company with 0 analysts and GICS classification as specialty chemicals is exactly the combination the exclusion-zone doctrine targets. No institutional buyer can act even after finding the signal.

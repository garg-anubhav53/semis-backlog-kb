## 1. VARIANT PERCEPTION GAP

**The order book math tells a story the headline guidance does not.**

FY2026 revenue guidance is stated as "exceeding €100M." This sounds like a target. It is actually already covered more than 1.5x by the existing order book before a single new H2 order is taken.

**Reconstruction of the order book entering H2 2026:**

| Period | Order Intake | Revenue | Order Book (end) |
|---|---|---|---|
| FY2025 close | — | — | €51M (confirmed) |
| Q1 2026 | €13.6M | €18.2M | €49M (confirmed) |
| Q2 2026 (est.) | ~€68.1M | ~€20M (est.) | ~€97M (estimated) |

Q2 2026 intake is derived: €81.7M H1 confirmed − €13.6M Q1 confirmed = €68.1M single quarter. This is not an estimate of intake — the H1 total is drawn from the July 7, 2026 6-K verbatim. Q2 revenue of ~€20M is estimated conservatively; Q1 was €18.2M and Q1 is described by management as "generally the softest period in terms of order intake and revenue contribution."

**The coverage ratio:** H2 revenue needed to hit the >€100M FY guidance is approximately €62M (€100M − ~€38M H1). The estimated entering-H2 order book of ~€97M covers that by 1.56x. This is before any July or August orders, which management has explicitly stated are still shippable within FY2026: "Orders taken until July or August will still be recognized as revenue in the fiscal year, provided they can be produced and shipped before year-end."

**Upside scenario:** If SHMD ships even €85M of the ~€97M backlog in H2, FY2026 revenue lands at ~€123M — a 23%+ beat to the >€100M guidance floor. The order intake line (expected to be revised upward from €114M in tomorrow's update) would further reinforce this.

**Why hasn't consensus modeled this?**
- The €37M order comprising approximately 38% of Q2 intake was disclosed six days ago (July 7, 2026). No analyst has had a full business week to update a forward model.
- The company has not yet released preliminary Q2 revenue (expected tomorrow, July 14). Until that number prints, the Q2 order book endpoint remains an estimate.
- SHMD does not formally disclose a book-to-bill ratio. The BTB must be manually computed from 6-K disclosures. Implied H1 2026 BTB of ~2.15x is extraordinary relative to the sector threshold of 1.20x flagged in macro context as the "target cohort" — but it is invisible to any screener that looks for an explicit BTB disclosure.
- The company files as a foreign private issuer (6-K and 20-F), not on the standard 10-Q cadence. Cadence and format differences reduce the reach of automated SEC-scraping models used by many quant funds.

Foreign private issuer filing format (low weight).** The absence of quarterly 10-Q filings means the order-book-level detail embedded in 6-K press releases does not automatically populate the databases that drive consensus model refresh cycles. This is a friction asymmetry, not a fundamental one.

## 4. LANGUAGE QUALITY

**Verbatim from July 7, 2026 6-K (primary source):**

> "SCHMID Group N.V. ... announced today that it has secured a **repeat order** exceeding **EUR 37 million** from a **leading Chinese customer** for **advanced HDI-ML and mSAP production equipment**. The order is for supporting **capacity expansion** for **next-generation AI server boards and optical module applications**."

> "Order intake in the six months ended June 30, 2026 was EUR 44.3 million excluding this order and **EUR 81.7 million including it.**"

> "Following the recent acceleration in order intake, the Company is reviewing an **upward revision** of its guidance on order intake and expects to communicate revised quantitative guidance with its business update for the second quarter of 2026 on or about **July 14, 2026.**"

**Verbatim from Q4 2025 earnings call (advance payment structure):**

> "SCHMID typically receives about **30% of cash advance** from customers on most equipment orders, which helps to keep the working capital at a reasonable level."

> "When selling machines, customers' payments are typically divided into an advanced payment on receipt of order confirmation, an advance payment upon delivery and, when installation services are requested, a final payment after installation and customer acceptance of the services."

**Assessment of language quality:**

The language is specific and cash-backed, not promotional.

Four markers of specificity:
1. **"Repeat order"** — this is not a letter of intent or a framework agreement. The filing explicitly references "the first phase supplied by SCHMID in 2025" as completed. The customer has already received and accepted prior SCHMID equipment. This is verifiable.
2. **"Exceeding EUR 37 million"** — a floor amount, not a range. Presented in a liability-subject SEC filing (6-K). Any material misstatement is subject to Rule 10b-5.
3. **"HDI-ML and mSAP production equipment"** — precise equipment taxonomy (High Density Interconnect Multi-Layer and modified Semi-Additive Process), not generic "manufacturing equipment." This specificity has technical verification pathways.
4. **"Capacity expansion"** — the customer is adding to a running production line, not piloting. This implies the existing installed base is generating revenue, which provides collateral support for the order's legitimacy.

The 30% advance payment structure is the strongest cash-backing signal. On a €37M+ order, this implies approximately **€11M+ in cash** (or a binding payment obligation) received at order confirmation in Q2 2026. This amount should appear as a contract liability on the Q2 balance sheet. The advance payment mechanism is not a deposit receipt in the traditional sense — it is a binding contractual milestone payment from a customer who has already completed a prior delivery cycle with SCHMID. The 2025 annual filing noted "delays in receiving contractual advance payments have resulted in project postponements" — meaning the company tracks this metric as a leading indicator of execution risk and the absence of such delays on the current order is a positive signal.

**One genuine weakness:** No explicit delivery window is stated in the quoted language. Management's general statement ("orders taken until July or August will still be recognized as revenue in the fiscal year") provides a proxy, but the order-specific delivery schedule is not disclosed. This is normal for equipment suppliers under NDA but is a limitation on precision.

## 5. VERDICT PRE-SCORE

**Signal Fidelity: HIGH**

**Rationale:**

The investment signal rests on three pillars that are independently confirmable:

1. **Order book math is arithmetically clean.** Entering-H2 backlog of ~€97M covers the ~€62M H2 revenue needed to hit guidance by 1.56x, based on confirmed figures from two separate 6-K filings and one earnings call. No inference is required — only arithmetic.

2. **Catalysts are date-specific and imminent.** The Q2 guidance update occurs tomorrow (July 14). There is no ambiguity about when the market will be forced to update.

3. **The advance payment structure converts orders into cash.** The 30% down payment on a repeat order from an existing customer is a near-real-money signal, not a future promise. The ~€11M+ advance payment from the €37M order should appear on the Q2 balance sheet.

**Risks that cap signal fidelity below VERY HIGH:**

- **China concentration risk is material.** Approximately 50%+ of recent order intake appears China-linked. Any incremental export control action — particularly targeting PCB manufacturing equipment or specific Chinese customers in the AI server supply chain — could impair delivery of the €37M order and the broader backlog. This is not a low-probability tail; export control policy toward China's AI infrastructure buildout is an active and ongoing government program in both the US and EU. This risk cannot be dismissed as priced-in on a six-day-old order.

- **Production capacity step-change.** FY2026 requires shipping approximately €100M+ of equipment. H1 2025 revenue was only €16.9M (company was in trough). The implied H2 2026 run rate of €60M+ would be the highest half-year shipment in the company's recent history. Management has not guided explicitly on production capacity constraints. The $20M convertible notes raised simultaneously with the €37M order announcement suggest capital was needed to fund the production cycle — which is normal but confirms the execution demand is real.

- **FX.** Revenue is EUR-denominated; shares trade in USD. EUR/USD fluctuation affects both reported USD revenue and equity valuation in USD terms.

**Pre-score: BUY WATCH — HIGH signal fidelity, binary catalyst July 14 tomorrow, size for China tail risk**

The order-book math is one of the more concrete near-term revenue visibility setups in the small-cap semiconductor equipment universe. The asymmetry is real and closes within 24 hours. Position sizing should reflect China concentration risk and the company's early stage as a post-IPO issuer with limited public track record. The July 14 6-K is the decisive read: if it confirms revised guidance above €130M order intake and Q2 revenue tracking toward €20M+, the H2 revenue conversion thesis has full setup.

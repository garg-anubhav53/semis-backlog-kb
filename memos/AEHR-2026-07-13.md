The variant perception gap: consensus may have modeled a slow, linear recovery from the optical trough without accounting for the discrete, order-based nature of Aehr's revenue. Backlog is the lead indicator, not trailing revenue, and the 228% QoQ backlog increase (from $11.8M to $38.7M) happened in a single quarter — the kind of inflection that quarterly model updates often lag by one to two reporting cycles.

## Q2 — ASYMMETRY SOURCE

Several credible factors may have caused the market to underweight this signal:

1. **Optical trough narrative dominance.** With revenue down 44% YoY, the dominant frame entering Q3 FY2026 was cyclical trough. Investors anchored to the trailing revenue number rather than the forward backlog, which is a standard behavioral bias in capital-equipment names.

2. **Miscategorization / coverage gap.** Aehr sits in a narrow niche — wafer-level burn-in and test — and is not categorized as an AI infrastructure play despite explicit management commentary naming hyperscale data center AI processors and silicon photonics as demand drivers. The macro consensus flags large-cap WFE names (TSMC, Samsung, Lam, AMAT); sub-$500M names with 4–6 analysts do not receive systematic institutional attention. With only ~4–6 sell-side analysts, a single coverage gap or missed update is enough to leave the order inflection unmodeled.

3. **Signal buried in backlog definition.** The $38.7M figure appears in the balance-sheet footnotes and MD&A as a "backlog" disclosure, not as a P&L line. Screens and models that run off revenue or EBITDA miss it entirely.

That said, a candid note: by the July 2026 date of this memo, two earnings cycles have elapsed since the April call. The asymmetry window is narrowing as each quarterly print forces update.

**Order language assessment — specificity vs. promotional:**

The extract and call commentary reference: "confirmed purchase orders," "scheduled for shipment within 12 months," and named application verticals (AI processors, silicon photonics, silicon carbide). The call is reported to include a $14M AI customer order and a major new silicon photonics win with delivery windows implied by the 12-month backlog definition. The company's backlog definition is explicitly tied to "confirmed purchase orders" — not letters of intent, not verbal commitments.

This language is moderately specific and operationally grounded. The backlog definition anchors it to cash-backed POs with defined delivery schedules. What is absent from the public extract: named customers (hyperscale unnamed), deposit receipt confirmation, or specific quarter-by-quarter delivery schedules. The language is more specific than promotional, but short of the gold standard (named buyer + cash deposit + pinned delivery quarter).

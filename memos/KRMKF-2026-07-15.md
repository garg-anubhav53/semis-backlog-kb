## QUESTION 1 — SIGNAL: Is there real order momentum?

**VERDICT: MODERATE-STRONG (with critical nuance)**

The headline numbers are exceptional but require decomposition:

**H1 FY2026 Revenue: £15.0m (+305% YoY from £3.7m)**
- Advanced Imaging: £10.8m — includes $25m Siemens Healthineers enablement payment (first milestone). Underlying Advanced Imaging ex-Siemens: £2.5m (+41% YoY). Genuine growth, smaller base.
- CBRN Detection: £4.3m (vs £2.0m, +115%) — THIS is the clean signal. Genuine product revenue from government customers. No one-time distortion.

**H2 FY2026: £8.8m new orders confirmed**
**FY2026 Full Year guidance: £27.2m revenue, £2.15m PBT (profitable)**

**Milestone-based revenue recognition (critical):** The Siemens deal ($37.5m total) breaks down as:
- FY2025 recognized: £16.5m (bulk upfront payment)
- FY2026 guided: $11.7m (~£9.3m)
- FY2027 expected: $2.5m (~£2.0m) — CLIFF

The headline revenue will drop significantly in FY2027 as Siemens milestones tail off. The genuine recurring signal is in CBRN (+115%), underlying imaging (+41%), and UK/US government framework drawdowns.

**What keeps signal real:** Pre-qualification on UK Home Office £84m/4yr framework + US DoD $8.2bn/10yr IDIQ creates structured order visibility. CBRN segment has no Siemens distortion — every pound is recurring product revenue from defense/security customers.

**No explicit backlog amount or B2B ratio disclosed** (AIM companies typically don't).

Signal classification: **MODERATE** on true recurring basis; **STRONG** on FY2026 numbers but must discount Siemens milestone tail-off in FY2027.

## QUESTION 3 — MOAT

**Moat classification: QUALIFIED (CBRN) + ERODING MONOPOLY (CZT medical)**

### Evidence for moat:

**Government qualification lock-in (CBRN — durable):**
- UK Government Radiological Nuclear Detection Framework: pre-qualified in ALL THREE categories (handheld, wearable, large static); £84m ceiling/4yr; "uniquely positioned"; "one of a limited number of pre-approved suppliers"
- US DoD IDIQ: pre-qualified for CBRN detection; $8.2bn/10yr framework
- Government CBRN qualification involves extensive testing, MIL-spec compliance, security clearance requirements. Qualification cycle for a new supplier = typically 18-36 months. This is a durable QUALIFIED moat.

**CZT supply position (medical — eroding):**
- "Only independent commercial-scale producer of CZT globally" — management claim of MONOPOLY status among independent suppliers
- However: The Siemens Healthineers Enablement Agreement **transfers 15 of 174 furnaces** and proprietary CZT manufacturing IP to Siemens — effectively creating a competitor from their largest customer
- Canon and others are entering photon-counting CT with their own CZT capability
- The medical imaging CZT moat is being actively transferred away; this was likely a financial necessity (survival capital)

**Net moat assessment:**
- CBRN detection segment: QUALIFIED moat — pre-approved government supplier with multi-year frameworks; requalification window 18-36 months for any new entrant
- Medical imaging segment: MONOPOLY claim is weakening; Siemens transfer + Canon entry = degrading moat
- The durable edge lives in CBRN, not imaging

**Strongest moat quote:** "As one of a limited number of pre-approved suppliers, the company is 'uniquely positioned to support the Home Office's mission to strengthen national security.'" + US DoD IDIQ pre-qualification.

## QUESTION 4 — ASYMMETRY: Why is this signal not in the price?

**Specific structural reasons:**

1. **AIM + OTC Pink = Dual exclusion:** No US institutional can hold OTC Pink positions under most fund mandates; most UK institutions require FTSE inclusion. Kromek falls into the gap between both.

2. **Revenue distortion masks trajectory:** The Siemens enablement revenue (£16.5m in FY2025, ~£9m in FY2026, £2m in FY2027) makes the P&L nearly unreadable using standard screens. A screener sees FY2026 as a one-time spike and assigns low quality multiple. The underlying CBRN growth is invisible behind the noise.

3. **Zero analyst coverage:** No initiation = no price target = no institutional research memo = no fund can buy per compliance

4. **Classification gap:** "Radiation detector" maps to medical devices or instruments in GICS, not defense or semiconductor materials. This means it doesn't appear on: defense tech screens, semiconductor supply chain screens, OR specialty materials screens. It appears on no institutional screen at all.

5. **Prior unprofitability:** Years of operating losses (FY2024 and prior) kept value investors away. FY2026 is the first year of guided profitability — the inflection is new.

**Named asymmetry:** AIM-listing + revenue distortion from milestone accounting + GICS void + prior loss history = company generating £27.2m revenue with government qualification moat trading at <3x projected revenue. No institution has a pathway to discover or act on this.

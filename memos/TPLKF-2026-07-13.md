## QUESTION 2 — ASYMMETRY SOURCE

There are three credible, specific reasons the market may have underweighted this:

**1. EBITDA miss masked the order signal.** Q1 EBITDA crashed from EUR 8.2M to EUR 1.4M (margin: 2.5% vs 13.9%). Negative earnings screens would flag the profit miss; order intake screens would catch the surge, but negative sentiment from margin compression typically drives short-term institutional selling that suppresses re-rating.

**2. Non-standard delivery timelines confused extrapolation.** Management stated explicitly on the earnings call: *"First orders for HBM were received in Metrology, with delivery agreed for 2027, so the usual pattern of four to eight months in Metrology doesn't apply to these orders."* Sell-side models built on standard 4–8 month Metrology conversion cycles would systematically misplace HBM orders into 2026 revenue rather than 2027, understating the backlog duration.

**3. Coverage thinness and OTC illiquidity.** Eight analysts cover the stock. TPLKF trades OTC with low ADR volume; US-based buyside has minimal visibility. HBM as an end market for acoustic metrology is a structural new application — existing coverage teams built their models on older SAM use cases (DRAM, logic), not HBM inspection.

## 5. Key Uncertainties

**Risk 1: Design-win-to-volume lag risk (highest probability)**
The "multiple tier-1 InP photonics design wins" language explicitly says they "position IQE for future volume production." In compound semiconductors, the lag from design win to production volumes is typically 12-24 months. If the tier-1 wins were secured in late 2025/early 2026, volume may not hit IQE's revenue line until FY2027. The FY2026 photonics growth story may be primarily driven by Tower and defence contracts, with the broader InP inflection being a 2027 event. This would mean FY2026 meets guidance (>20%) but disappoints relative to bull case expectations, and the re-rating needs 2027 financials.

**Risk 2: Wireless remains a structural drag, not cyclical**
Wireless revenue fell 40% in FY2025 to £40.1M. IQE's wireless segment serves mobile handset RF front-end modules (GaAs pHEMT/HBT). If this decline is structural (design migration to silicon-on-insulator or other alternatives) rather than purely cyclical, the photonics ramp must overcome an ongoing offset. No analyst commentary available on wireless structural vs. cyclical split.

**Risk 3: MACOM relationship opacity**
The MACOM "strategic partnership" is described as a £30M investment. It is unclear from public disclosures whether MACOM is an IQE customer, a co-development partner, or a purely financial investor. If MACOM's investment is primarily financial (taking a discounted position during a fundraise) rather than supply-chain strategic, it provides less demand visibility than the market might assume. The investment at 19.8p — significantly below any plausible intrinsic value at current multiples — suggests MACOM saw strategic value, but the commercial terms are opaque.

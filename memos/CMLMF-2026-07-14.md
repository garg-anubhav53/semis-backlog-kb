## Q1. SIGNAL — Is there real order momentum?

**Short answer: Soft directional signal. No hard backlog figure. Proceed with caution.**

The order signal is real but thin on quantification:

- H2 FY26 revenue ~£11.27m vs H1 FY26 £9.18m — sequential improvement of +18%. This is internally consistent and not trivial.
- H2 FY26 also exceeded H2 FY25 on an absolute basis — the first year-over-year period improvement in FY26, confirming the trough was H1 FY26 (down 27% YoY).
- Management language: "improving customer inventory levels and growing order activity" — inventory destocking cycle appears to have passed.
- "The forward order book remains healthy and serves to underpin the outlook for further growth across the next financial year." (FY26 Full Year Results, June 2026)
- No book-to-bill ratio disclosed. No order book value in £ disclosed. No customer deposit line in available summaries.
- NRE income from GNSS contract recognized on design milestones — this is contract-guaranteed cash, not open-market orders, and contributes to H2 FY26 strength.
- GNSS contract: >$30M over 12 years, design phase 2-3 years. Product-phase revenue is not in the FY27 period; it represents a medium-term inflection, not a near-term backlog fill.

**Assessment:** The H2 FY26 inflection is the signal. Sequential revenue recovery and the first YoY-positive half in the cycle qualifies as an emerging order momentum signal. However, the absence of a disclosed backlog number or B2B ratio is a meaningful gap. This passes as a directional signal sufficient to proceed; it does not pass as a hard B2B above 1.20 confirmation. Flag accordingly.

## Q3. MOAT

**Primary moat evidence — GNSS contract (July 2025):**

> "CML's ability to meet stringent manufacturing requirements and provide long-term supply chain resilience are key attributes [that secured the multi-year commitment]."

> "A 12-year design and supply agreement with a prominent global manufacturer of industrial GNSS equipment."

> "The initial two-to-three-year design phase is well underway and will pave the way for substantial product sales."

**Moat analysis:**

The term "sole-source" does not appear explicitly in available RNS summaries. However, the structure of the agreement implies functional sole-source status through design-in lock-in:

- A 12-year design-and-supply agreement is not structured as a competitive supply arrangement. It is structured as a platform partnership — CML's RF IC will be designed into the customer's GNSS platform architecture during a 2-3 year design phase. Once that design-in is complete, re-qualifying a competitor requires restarting the multi-year design cycle at the customer's cost and timeline risk.
- "Stringent manufacturing requirements" is qualification-moat language. The customer's use of this phrase to explain why CML was selected signals that not all suppliers can meet the bar — this is implicit sole-source.
- 12-year duration is extraordinary. Standard supply agreements in electronics run 3-5 years. A 12-year commitment signals the customer views CML as a long-term embedded supplier, not a commodity vendor on a rotational basis.
- "Long-term supply chain resilience" as a key attribute suggests the customer has explicitly priced in the risk of re-qualification and has chosen to avoid it.

**Broader moat assessment:**

CML operates in niche RF/mixed-signal ICs for positioning, communications, and storage — markets where customer certification cycles, regulatory approvals (GPS/GNSS accuracy certification, industrial safety ratings), and long product lifecycles (industrial/defense GNSS equipment has 10-20 year product lives) create natural barriers to entry and switching. The company's AIM listing history spans decades, suggesting a stable niche supply base rather than a commodity competitor.

**Classification: QUALIFIED**

Rationale: The evidence strongly implies design-in lock-in consistent with functional sole-source status, but the word "sole-source" is absent from available public disclosure. A direct read of the full GNSS contract RNS or annual report could upgrade this to MONOPOLY for the GNSS platform. The moat is real; the classification is constrained by documentation access, not by evidence to the contrary.

## Q4. ASYMMETRY — Why is this not in the price?

The share price at 265p sits 29% below the 52-week high of 374p (the GNSS announcement spike) and represents a reversion to pre-announcement levels. The market has effectively priced out the GNSS contract. Structural reasons:

**1. Liquidity exclusion (primary):** At <$400K/day USD average volume, no institution managing more than $50-100M AUM can build a meaningful position without moving the stock substantially. The float is too small to absorb institutional interest. The GNSS spike to 374p demonstrates this — even modest buying drove a 40% move. The same dynamic means no institution can enter cleanly, so most do not attempt it.

**2. No US analyst coverage (zero):** The 3 analysts covering CML are UK/AIM boutiques. Their research does not distribute into US institutional channels, Bloomberg terminal searches, or major research aggregators used by US funds. The GNSS contract received no US sell-side note. It effectively did not happen for the US institutional universe.

**3. Revenue decline narrative:** FY26 revenue was down 11% YoY. Any screener running trailing revenue growth filters would exclude CMLMF. The H2 inflection (sequential +18%, YoY positive for H2) is invisible to backward-looking screens.

**4. GNSS revenue timing mismatch:** The $30M contract is real, but product revenue begins in 2-3 years (i.e., ~FY28-FY29). Investors anchored to next-twelve-months financials see NRE milestone revenue, not the product ramp. The long time horizon is a feature for a long-term holder and a deterrent for anyone with quarterly performance pressure.

**5. AIM/Pink Sheets structural exclusion:** The majority of US equity mandates explicitly exclude AIM-listed companies or Pink Sheets OTC. This is not a judgment call — it is a hard policy exclusion. The universe of eligible buyers is structurally restricted.

**6. No AI narrative:** CML's end markets (industrial GNSS, satellite communications, storage) have no AI/data center association. The RF IC space is not being re-rated in the current cycle because it is not in the conversation. A company doing similar work with an AI framing (edge inference, satellite AI routing) would trade at a material premium.

**Net effect:** A company with a 12-year $30M+ contract from a named global GNSS manufacturer, net cash of ~$14M against a $56M market cap (implying ~$42M enterprise value for the operating business), returning to growth, trading at roughly 1.6x EV/Sales — and no one in the relevant institutional universe can legally or practically buy it.

## Q5. CATALYST — What forces consensus to update?

**Primary catalyst: FY27 H1 Interim Results (~October/November 2026)**

CML's fiscal year ends March 31. The H1 FY27 interim (covering April-September 2026) would be released approximately October-November 2026 — within the 1-3 quarter window. This is the first data point that will show whether the "return to revenue growth in FY27" guidance is tracking. Given H2 FY26 already exceeded H2 FY25, H1 FY27 vs H1 FY26 (£9.18m, the trough) sets an achievable year-over-year comparison.

If H1 FY27 revenue is, say, £11-12m (a reasonable estimate given H2 FY26 run-rate), the YoY growth rate would be 20%+, and the full-year FY27 trajectory would become visible. That is a consensus-forcing data point for the 3 existing analysts to upgrade estimates, and a trigger event that could attract a 4th or 5th analyst.

**Secondary catalyst: GNSS design phase milestone disclosure (timing uncertain, but likely CY2026-CY2027)**

The GNSS design phase is 2-3 years from the July 2025 agreement. A key design review milestone was already passed (disclosed in FY26 results). The next formal milestone disclosure — completion of design phase, transition to qualification, or first volume purchase order — would force a revaluation of the $30M contract from "possible" to "contracted." No specific date is public, but design-phase completion would logically occur in 2027-2028.

**Tertiary catalyst: Additional long-term supply agreement announcement**

Management cited "a healthy level of opportunity in the pipeline" and "an expanding product portfolio." A second multi-year design-and-supply agreement announcement would re-rate the business model from "GNSS contract company" to "specialist RF IC platform supplier with recurring long-term design wins." That narrative shift would be a material re-rating event.

**Most actionable for position timing:** FY27 H1 results, approximately October/November 2026.

3. 3 analysts vs ≤2 criterion — minor but documented.

5. Liquidity: <$400K/day means position sizing is constrained even if thesis is correct.

**Upgrade to CANDIDATE if:** FY27 H1 results show YoY revenue growth (confirming guidance), or if a GNSS design milestone + purchase order framework is disclosed.

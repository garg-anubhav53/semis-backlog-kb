- "Geopolitical uncertainty" creating booking delays

Management's own words confirm the weakness: "complete absence of new CVD system orders contradicts management's narrative about growing traction in silicon carbide (SiC) and aerospace markets."

## 3. MOAT: Is this company structurally hard to displace?

**Classification: UNKNOWN (leaning COMPETING)**

No sole-source, qualification lock-in, regulatory certification, or long-term supply agreement language was found in any indexed extract of the Q1 2026 filing.

What the filing describes:
- FirstNano product brand (CVD systems for SiC electronics, EV batteries, aerospace CMC)
- Single remaining business segment after SDC divestiture
- Target markets: SiC power electronics, aerospace ceramics, EV battery materials

**Why moat is weak/unknown:**
- CVD equipment for SiC R&D is made by multiple suppliers (Aixtron, Veeco, Entegris, smaller players)
- The SiC market deflation from Chinese supply means customers are NOT capacity-expanding — they're reducing capex
- FirstNano is a brand name, not a proprietary process protected by patents or regulatory certification visible in the filing
- Bookings "pressured by geopolitical uncertainty, reduced U.S. government funding for universities, and a slower pace of adoption" — these are demand-side macro headwinds that won't go away quickly

Without finding explicit customer qualification language, sole-source language, or MIL-SPEC/ITAR certifications, the moat cannot be confirmed.

## QUESTION 1 — VARIANT PERCEPTION GAP

**What the order data signals about the next 2–4 quarters:**

The order book and book-to-bill trajectory constitute a classic inflection setup that consensus earnings models almost certainly have not fully captured. The specific numbers:

- FY2025 order intake: **£225.9M** (+24% reported, +28% constant currency) against revenue of **£229.7M** (-7% reported). This divergence — orders accelerating hard while revenue still declined — is the definitional lag signal. The book-to-bill recovered from **0.73x** at the FY2024 trough to **0.83x** full-year FY2024, then to **0.98x** FY2025.
- Year-end FY2025 order book: **£115.8M**. This backlog is roughly 6 months of FY2025 revenue run-rate, providing direct visibility into H1 2026 revenue without requiring new orders.
- Q1 2026 order intake: **£79.1M** (+48% constant currency YoY). Book-to-bill jumped to **1.53x** in Q1 2026 vs 1.07x in Q1 2025.
- Contract liabilities increased **£16.4M** due to a large semiconductor equipment customer prepayment — cash received in advance, not a forecast.

The arithmetic is straightforward: a 1.53x BTB in Q1 implies orders are running at roughly £316M annualised, against FY2025 revenue of £229.7M — a ~38% implied organic revenue growth rate if orders convert at historical lead times (typically 3–9 months for capital equipment sub-systems). Even with conservative assumptions (some order slippage, RF wind-down headwind of ~£24M), FY2026 revenue should exit the year meaningfully above FY2025. Consensus models for a company that printed -7% revenue in FY2025 are statistically unlikely to have modelled +25–35% revenue growth in FY2026 without a forced update from an earnings print.

**Verdict: High-confidence gap. The BTB inflection from 0.73x trough to 1.53x in four quarters is the signal. Consensus was set during the trough and has not been reset by actual reported revenue growth yet.**

## QUESTION 2 — ASYMMETRY SOURCE

Several credible, specific reasons exist for why the market has not fully priced this:

**1. Thin analyst coverage (6 analysts).** Six analysts is below the "edge thesis" threshold of four in some frameworks, but it is thin enough that model refresh discipline is poor. The consensus 12-month price target of approximately **1,760p** sits roughly **10% below the current share price of ~1,958p** — meaning even the formal bull consensus is stale relative to where the market has taken the stock on order momentum alone. This is a tell: market participants have partially priced the inflection, but sell-side models have not yet caught up.

**2. Revenue decline in FY2025 obscured the order signal.** Any screens filtering for revenue growth would have excluded XPP entirely in FY2025 (revenue -7%). The BTB data is in the MD&A and order book disclosures, not the headline P&L, making it invisible to screens and many generalist investors.

**3. Operational noise from restructuring.** The exit of the RF power division (revenue £24.3M in FY2025, 3-year wind-down) and the Malaysia factory consolidation/China plant closure created accounting complexity and distracted investor attention from the core semiconductor equipment order acceleration. The £16.4M prepayment (booked as a contract liability) is buried in the balance sheet notes, not highlighted in headline results.

**4. Miscategorisation.** XPP is classified as an industrial/electronic components company on LSE screens, not as a semiconductor equipment sub-system supplier. It is a UK-listed Singapore-incorporated small-cap at ~£542M market cap — structurally under-followed by US-based WFE sector analysts who cover Applied Materials, Lam Research, and KLA but do not drill into their supply chains. The company is not labelled an "AI play" despite being a direct beneficiary of the same capex cycle.

**5. EV/Sales context.** At a P/S of approximately 1.7–2.1x (sources vary slightly) and with revenue likely inflecting to +25–35% growth, XPP is within the "below 2x EV/Sales with improving order signals" asymmetry zone identified in the macro KB. This would be re-rated meaningfully higher if it were US-listed and labelled a semiconductor equipment supplier.

**Verdict: Asymmetry is credible and specific — not promotional. The thinness of coverage, revenue-decline screen exclusion, restructuring noise, and UK/industrial miscategorisation together explain the gap.**

## ORDER LANGUAGE QUALITY ASSESSMENT

**Quoted from FY2025 MD&A (per KB extract):**
> "strengthening demand momentum, particularly in the semiconductor manufacturing equipment sector, with the company well positioned for the next upcycle in that market"

This phrasing is moderately specific (sector named, directional language) but not maximally specific (no named customers, no delivery windows cited in the MD&A).

**However, the balance sheet note is the higher-quality signal:**
> Contract liabilities: Increased £16.4M due to large customer prepayment for semiconductor equipment customers

This is cash-backed. A prepayment from a named customer class ("semiconductor equipment customers") means:
- Cash has been received
- A contract exists with delivery obligations
- The customer is a semiconductor equipment OEM (almost certainly one of the major WFE OEMs — Applied Materials, Lam Research, KLA, or similar)

**Q1 2026 RNS language (from Investing.com):**
> Order intake of £79.1M (+48% constant currency); book-to-bill of 1.53x

This is quantified, reported, and auditable. It is not promotional language.

**Overall assessment:** The MD&A language is sector-specific and directionally credible, not purely promotional. The contract liability increase is the strongest proof point — it is a GAAP balance sheet item representing actual cash received. The Q1 BTB of 1.53x is the quantitative confirmation. Together, the language quality grades as **specific and cash-backed** at the balance sheet level, even if the MD&A narrative stops short of naming specific customers or delivery windows.
